Bathroom 1

The renovation of St. Catherines, Exeter, Devon

Focus on the story behind getting the family bathroom done at St. Catherines, Exeter.

The bathroom was started just as Covid 19 lock down kicked in, which presented us with some challenges. I decided to do it myself after our plumber abandoned us.

This is the original state of the bathroom. Actually it doesn't look too bad in this picture. Definately dated though. Take a closer look at the sink. The tiles that cover the boxed in section at the end of the bath are falling off. The bath is is caste iron with a lot of blemishes. This photo taken from the original house details cleverly hides a step.

On entering the bathroom the first thing you noticed was a step (arrowed in this picture). The reason for this remained a mystery until we took the false floor up. I was dreading what I would find.

And here's how it looks now...

And this is how we got here...

Start by removing all the tiles. Ended up with pretty rough walls.
Smash up the iron bath; it's the only way to get these things out, they are incrediably heavy.
Bath gone. Remove wall paper as well with stripper.

Manged to get the toilet in (soil pipe and all) within the weekend. Almost got the bath done as well but I need a few more bits to get that up and running. Ok, soil pipe looks a bit wonky, but I'll sort that out when I replace the rest of the cast iron soil vent.

Started tiling.

Bath easy to slot back and forth. Although it leaked anyway because I forgot plumbers tape on one of the tapes.

Get rid of old pipe work where bath was and run under floor boards for sink (to be installed), bath and toilet. This tool pretty much all day, made worse because one of the Ts leaked. This is because the end of the pipe was as delivered, i.e. not cut which gives it a nice finish. Instead it was sharp and cut the seal as I pushed it in.

Finally floor boards down as well.

Didn't really want to drill a 40mm hole through the wall, so wondered if I could use the old hole. Still had the old lead pipe stuck in there but managed to figure out a way to knock it out. Unfortunately it took my pretty much all day to get the waste for show and sink ready.

Prep the window sill for tiling by building a frame with tile board. I bought it from Wickes thinking it was the same as cement board but infact it unusal. Very light and easy to cut because it is infact a sandwich of foam with a thing layer of cement board on either side. Great stuff to work with.

Finished tiling sill and around window. More tiling in shower area. Picture to come.

Radiator arrived, this is where it will be installed.

Start lining the floor with tileboard. This stuff is great. It's foam sandwitched in what appeares to be thin cement board with renforcement fibres. It's much easier to handle that cement board.

Pipework for the shower ready.

Shower mixer in, and more tiling done. More done even since this photo.

The floors ready for adhsive and the shower tray and waste is in and tested.

Plastering to level the wall a bit more before completing the tiling so the basin can go back.
This should reesult in the waste pipes being hidden behind the tiles as well.

Half floor stuck down and grouted. Also managed to get some grouting down on the wall tiles.

Base wood frame
Bit extra round the edges so I can tile the tray surround later
Ply wood x 2
Tray with seal to tuck under tiles
Boarder tiles

Took most of the day, but coouldn't afford to get this wrong.

Toilet and basin are in for good. Shower next and then all the finishing touches.

Get the bath panel in, looks a lot better.
