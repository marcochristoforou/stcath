Automation

The renovation of St. Catherines, Exeter, Devon

Focus on the smart home automation implemented at St. Catherines, Exeter.

I've gone for maximum automation using a variety of products based mainly round Google.

Front Gates

The front gates were already partially automated using BFT Thalia P with a key fob remote control.

BFT Thalis P Instruction Manual

All I needed to do is work out a way to control them over the internet and with voice control via Google Home Assistant. There's quite a lot out there, varying in price quote significantly.

In the end I opted for Remootio, coming out at $119. I could have got it cheaper had I realised I didn't need the power supply.

Remootio Intallation Manual

It's ok, but it's quite a lot of money for what you get, esepcially when compared to the garage door opener (see below). On the other hand it was a hell of a lot cheaper than its competitors. I think the app could be a bit nicer to look at and the install instructions were a bit vague in places so someone non-technical might struggle. On the other hand it does work very well.

So now I can open and close the gates by voice command and from my phone. You can set up automatic open as the car arrives and close as it leaves, but I haveN't done this yet.

Garage Door

The garage door was also already partially automated using Gliderol GRD

Glidermatic GRD Owner Manual

Once again, there's a lot to choose from but basically all you need is something that switches a relay. I already had a bluetooth module wired in.

I eventually found a very cheap item at only £25 (others were around £150) which I felt was worth a punt for the price. In the end I opted for Meross (Flysocks) MSG100, at £25 from Amazon. This turned out to be a great buy and I later realised I could have used this for the front gates as well. Admittedly it doesn't have quite the same level of functionality but is only missing the auto open function. It also doesn't inetergrate with Siri. I don't use Siri but it means the shortcut isn't quite a nice on the iPhone.

Meross (Flysocks) MSG100 User Manual

So now I can open and close the garage door or gets its status by voice command via Google Home or on my phone anywhere. It will tell when when the doors are opened or closed and I can see them via my Google cameras (see below).

Security

Living in the city centre feels a little disconcerting, and the house itself isn't that secure with single plane leaded light windows. We don't expect any unvited visitors either. So I thought I'd try some cameras and selected the Google Nest Cam Outdoor. Generally I like Google products, they tend to work well and look good.

I git two of these, one for the front gate and another for the front door. They will notify you when there is any motion or when it detects people. You can also set zones of activity, but you have to subscribe to the service, but it;s only £50 per year.

This is Schlage Encode wifi deadbolt locks. I really like them, but that are not cheap and I had to get them imported from the U.S. In fact you can't get anything like this in the U.K. They add another level of security to the back and front doors. They allow us to lock the door remotely. For example if we've left the front door on the latch. They also automatically lock at 10pm. Each person can have there own code. They're good quality and I think this particular style looks good on the house.

I installed a Google Nest Hello. On the front gate. Along with the automatic lock we'll be able to control entry to the premises. For conveience I've put a Nest Hub in the hall so the image of the person appears there, although you can use a phone.

It's a pretty cool device as it will learn familiar faces. So as it captures faces you can label them so it recognises people. It will then subsequently annouce people it knows.

This is the external lock that I've installed on the gate. Along with the Nest Hello it gives us a way to control access. The gate lock turns off over night which means no one get get in. When the gate is on a button lights on the outside of the gate. Pressing the button unlocks the lock and allows entry. The lock automatically locks itself when the gate closes. The gate is pushed closed by a pneumatic device.

Heating and Hot Water

For smart heating and hot water I've gone for Hive. Actually, it's just what my plumber suggested. In fact there are a few choices here as well, Google do a version.

Hive integrates well with Google Home Assistant. Voice control works but you can also control the components through Google Home app, which you can't do with the openers described above.

Lighting

I've decided to make a few lights automated so they come on when motion is detected. There's one in the hall, landing, lounge and porch.

Wifi Network

This is pretty crucial to all the automation components. Up till now I made do with some wifi extenders that I've had for quite a few years, strategically placed, and some old BT router that I'm using as wifi access points.

I've now decided to upgrade and set up a mesh network. I looked at quite a few options and in the end settled on Google Nest Wifi. At time of writing I've got the router and one access point. Already Netflix access is much faster. The Nest Points are also Google Home speakers with Google Assistant. I'll get at least one more; I need to cover a wifi black spot at the kitchen/utility end of the house on the ground floor.

Safety

I've selected Google Nest smoke and carbon monoxide detector. You can link these, which is required by building regulations. I've also gone for the mains wired version.

Nest Protect (Wired 230V) User Guide

Interface

To interact with my various automations I use Google Nest Wifi Point (above) which also works as a speaker with Google Assistant. However, where I don't need another access point (which are expensive) I have Home Speaker (free from BT as a sorry) and a couple of Nest Mini's (one free with Google Active subscription).

Convenience

Mows the lawn regularly. The idea with this is little and often rather than waiting for the grass to get long. Also works with Google Assistant.
